Agentforce Discovery — One‑Page Interview Scripts (per stakeholder)

Use these short, role-specific scripts for 30–60 minute discovery sessions. Each script includes objective, suggested duration, warm-up, prioritized questions, follow-ups, and wrap-up actions. Run sessions as conversation — start with goals, walk a “happy path” scenario, then probe edge cases and exceptions.

1) Executive Sponsor / Product Owner

Objective: Confirm business goals, success criteria, budget/timeline, major constraints. Duration: 30 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Quick intro, confirm role and decision authority for Agentforce.

Core questions (20 min)

  1. What is the primary business outcome you expect from Agentforce? (revenue, cost, compliance, retention)
  2. What are the top 3 success metrics/KPIs and target values?
  3. What is the target launch date and major milestones we must hit?
  4. What budget or headcount constraints should we know about?
  5. Which existing initiatives must Agentforce align with or replace?
  6. What are non-negotiable regulatory or legal constraints?
  7. What would cause you to cancel or scale back the project?

Follow-ups / probes

  • Ask for historical baselines for the KPIs named.
  • Confirm escalation path and final decision maker(s).

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Confirm acceptance criteria owner and timing for a follow-up review.

2) Business Operations / Process Owner

Objective: Understand current workflows, pain points, KPIs, automations and approvals. Duration: 45 minutes

Warm-up (3 min)

  • Ask them to describe a current end-to-end process that Agentforce should support.

Core questions (30 min)

  1. Walk me step-by-step through the process we’re improving. Who does what, and when?
  2. What are frequent exceptions or failure modes?
  3. What data items are captured and where are they stored today?
  4. Which rules/validations/SLAs must be enforced?
  5. Are there approvals or escalations? Describe routing and time windows.
  6. What automations or notifications would save the most time?
  7. How do you currently measure success (reports, dashboards)?
  8. What reports must be available at day 1?

Follow-ups

  • Request process maps, sample records, and typical volumes.
  • Ask about required integrations and data owners.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Agree on artifacts to share (process maps, SLA docs) and deadlines.

3) Sales / Customer Success / Marketing

Objective: Capture how Agentforce supports customer-facing teams and metrics tied to revenue. Duration: 30–45 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Ask for a typical day-in-the-life scenario.

Core questions (25–30 min)

  1. Which user personas will use Agentforce and what are their top tasks?
  2. What fields and data do you need to capture for leads/customers?
  3. What integrations are required (CRM, email, telephony)?
  4. What automations (lead assignment, reminders, follow-ups) are essential?
  5. What reporting or dashboards would impact performance most?
  6. What data access controls are required? Are there sensitive fields?
  7. What mobile or offline needs exist for field teams?

Follow-ups

  • Ask for sample exports, CRM schema, and top 5 pain points.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Confirm prioritized MVP list from their perspective.

4) End Users / Frontline Agents (2–3 representatives per persona)

Objective: Validate tasks, usability, edge cases, and device constraints. Duration: 45 minutes

Warm-up (3 min)

  • Quick role intro and current daily challenges.

Core questions (30 min)

  1. What are your top 5 tasks in a typical day? Which are most time-consuming?
  2. Walk me through one task from start to finish (screens, tools, data).
  3. What errors or exceptions do you encounter most?
  4. Which fields/actions must be available on mobile vs desktop?
  5. How should notifications/assignments be delivered?
  6. What would make this tool frustrating or helpful to you?
  7. Any accessibility needs (font size, screen reader, keyboard nav)?

Follow-ups

  • Observe a quick live demo or screen-sharing of current tools (if possible).
  • Request sample user data and frequency of tasks.

Wrap-up (2–3 min)

  • Ask them to rate current pain (1–10) and suggest one “must-have” feature.

5) IT / Platform / Infrastructure

Objective: Capture hosting, deployment, integration, identity, and operational constraints. Duration: 45 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Confirm ownership of infrastructure and deployment responsibilities.

Core questions (30 min)

  1. Preferred hosting (cloud provider, on-prem, hybrid)? Any disallowed vendors?
  2. Required environments (dev/test/staging/prod) and branching/release process?
  3. Authentication and SSO requirements (IdP, SAML, OAuth, MFA)?
  4. Network constraints, allowed ports, VPN, proxy, and firewall rules?
  5. Backup, DR, RTO/RPO expectations?
  6. Monitoring/logging/alerting tools to integrate with?
  7. CI/CD pipeline requirements and review/approval gates?
  8. Expected concurrency and scalability targets.

Follow-ups

  • Request architecture standards, runbooks, and contact for deployment approvals.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Confirm environment provisioning timeline and access process.

6) Security / Compliance / Legal

Objective: Identify data classification, audit, encryption, retention, and compliance needs. Duration: 30 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Ask for any existing security policies or control matrices.

Core questions (20–25 min)

  1. What data classification applies to the data (PII, PHI, confidential)?
  2. Encryption requirements (at-rest, in-transit) and key management rules?
  3. Authentication & authorization model expectations (RBAC/ABAC)?
  4. Required compliance or certifications (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC2, ISO)?
  5. Audit logging requirements: which events, retention period, and storage?
  6. Penetration testing and security review cadence?
  7. Incident response and breach notification workflow and SLAs?

Follow-ups

  • Request security policy docs, contact for pen-test scheduling.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Identify gating security approvals for launch.

7) Data / Analytics

Objective: Define reporting needs, data models, retention, and warehouse integration. Duration: 30 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Confirm where analytics currently live (DW, BI tool).

Core questions (20 min)

  1. Which KPIs must be tracked and how are they defined?
  2. Required dashboards and who will consume them?
  3. Data retention, aggregation windows, and granularity required?
  4. Need for feeding data to DW/BI? Which tools (Snowflake, Redshift, BigQuery)?
  5. Real-time vs batch analytics needs?
  6. Data quality and lineage expectations?

Follow-ups

  • Ask for schema examples, sample queries, and existing ETL docs.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Agree on initial telemetry events and sample payload format.

8) Integration / API Owners (external systems)

Objective: Gather integration endpoints, auth, rate limits, and SLA expectations. Duration: 30 minutes

Warm-up (2 min)

  • Confirm API ownership and available documentation.

Core questions (20 min)

  1. Which systems must be integrated at launch? Provide API docs, endpoints.
  2. Preferred integration pattern: REST, SOAP, webhooks, message queue?
  3. Authentication method and credentials provisioning process?
  4. Rate limits, SLA, maintenance windows, and contact for outages?
  5. Data mapping expectations and canonical fields?
  6. Error handling and retry semantics you prefer?

Follow-ups

  • Request API keys, sandbox access, and sample responses.

Wrap-up (3 min)

  • Confirm timelines for providing access and test environments.

Notes for all sessions

  • Always request sample data exports (CSV/JSON), process maps, API docs, and any existing mockups.
  • Capture decisions, owners, and due dates in real time.
  • Record sessions (with permission) and annotate actionable follow-ups.
